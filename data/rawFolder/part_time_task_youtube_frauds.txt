DOCUMENT ID: I4C-ADV-004
TITLE: Advisory on Part-Time Job Scams, YouTube Like/Subscribe Frauds, and Pre-Paid Tasks
ISSUING AGENCY: Indian Cyber Crime Coordination Centre (I4C)
DATE OF ISSUE: Current Operating Version

1. OVERVIEW OF THE FRAUD
Fraudsters send unsolicited WhatsApp or Telegram messages offering easy part-time work from home, such as liking YouTube videos, rating Google Maps locations, or writing product reviews for daily payouts.

2. MODUS OPERANDI
- Initial Hook: Victims are paid small amounts initially for simple tasks to build trust.
- Pre-Paid Tasks: Scammers then introduce high-return "pre-paid task packages" or crypto investment modules requiring upfront deposits.
- Escalating Demands: Once the victim deposits money, fake negative balances or system errors are displayed, demanding additional payments to unlock previous earnings.

3. PREVENTIVE MEASURES & OFFICIAL STANCE
- Genuine companies never ask candidates to pay money upfront to secure a job or receive daily earnings.
- Exercise extreme caution when responding to unsolicited work-from-home offers from unknown international or domestic numbers.
- File complaints on the cybercrime portal with transaction screenshots and suspect account details.
