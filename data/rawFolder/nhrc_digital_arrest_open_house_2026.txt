Title: National Human Rights Commission Open House Discussion on Safeguarding Against Digital Arrest Scams (June 2026)

The National Human Rights Commission (NHRC) organized an open house discussion addressing the severe threat of digital arrest scams, which target the rights, dignity, and security of individuals, particularly senior citizens, retired officials, and professionals. 

Key insights and statistics highlighted during the discussion:
- Indians lost approximately Rs. 52,976 crore to cyber-enabled frauds over a six-year period, with nearly 8 percent of these losses attributed directly to digital arrest scams.
- Cybercriminals exploit personal data from data leaks, use AI tools, deepfakes, and operate through international scam compounds (particularly in Southeast Asia) supported by mule account networks.
- Expert recommendations include recognizing digital arrest as a distinct criminal offense under the law, introducing automated financial transaction "circuit breakers," regulating OTT communication platforms, enhancing victim compensation mechanisms, and improving inter-agency coordination across banking, telecom, and law enforcement sectors.
