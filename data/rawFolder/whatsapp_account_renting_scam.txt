ADVISORY: TAU/ADV/005 (Dated: 15th October)[cite: 24]
Subject: WhatsApp Web Account Renting Scam - Using Facebook & Instagram[cite: 24]
Issued By: National Cybercrime Threat Analytics Unit of 14C, Ministry of Home Affairs[cite: 24]

Overview:
* An emerging transnational crime trend involves Facebook and Instagram ads claiming users can earn automatic cash by linking their WhatsApp accounts[cite: 24].
* Ads redirect users to fraudulent web pages or Android APKs imitating legitimate earning platforms[cite: 24].
* Unsuspecting users are lured by high commissions and instructed to connect their accounts via QR codes, allowing threat actors to exploit WhatsApp's linked-device feature[cite: 24].
* Compromised accounts are rented out as "mule WhatsApp accounts" to scale illegal activities like fraud, scams, and dissemination of malicious content[cite: 24].

Modus Operandi:
* Advertisement & Redirection: Meta ads promise easy money, directing users to fake webpages or malicious APKs[cite: 24].
* Account Linking: Victims scan a QR code via their WhatsApp app, giving scammers continuous session access[cite: 24].
* Multi-Level Commission Scheme: Promotes pyramid structures (Level 1: 10% commission, Level 2: 5%, Level 3: 2%) to encourage users to onboard others and harvest more accounts[cite: 24].
* Abuse: Mule WhatsApp accounts are utilized for phishing, payment fraud, and recruitment for further mule services[cite: 24].

Precautions:
* Renting out WhatsApp accounts and receiving illicit funds can lead to severe legal consequences and arrest[cite: 24].
* Avoid installing APKs from unknown sources[cite: 24].
* Be cautious of Meta ads promising quick income, referral commissions, or automatic cash earnings[cite: 24].
* Periodically check "Linked Devices" in WhatsApp settings for suspicious sessions[cite: 24].
* Report account compromises or issues via the official WhatsApp support form[cite: 24].
