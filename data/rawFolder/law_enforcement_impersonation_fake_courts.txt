DOCUMENT ID: CBI-SC-ADV-006
TITLE: Advisory Against Fake Court Hearings, Summons, and Law Enforcement Impersonation
ISSUING AGENCY: Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) & Supreme Court Cyber Cell
DATE OF ISSUE: Current Operating Version

1. OVERVIEW OF THE FRAUD
Scammers impersonate judges, Supreme Court officials, CBI directors, or custom officials, sending fabricated notices, summons, or video-link court hearings to extort money from targets.

2. MODUS OPERANDI
- Creation of Fake Setups: Fraudsters use studio setups resembling courtrooms or police stations during video calls to intimidate victims.
- Threat of Imminent Action: Targets are told that a non-bailable warrant or Interpol red notice has been issued against them for parcel contraband or financial crimes.
- Settlement Extortion: Victims are forced to pay immediate "court fees," "bail bonds," or "settlement amounts" to avoid arrest.

3. PREVENTIVE MEASURES & OFFICIAL STANCE
- Courts and law enforcement agencies never conduct judicial hearings, trials, or settlement negotiations over WhatsApp, Skype, or phone calls.
- Legitimate legal notices are served through official postal channels or registered court process servers, never via direct messaging apps.
- Verify any claims directly through official district court or agency websites.
