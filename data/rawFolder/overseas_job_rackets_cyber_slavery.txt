DOCUMENT ID: MEA-ADV-003
TITLE: Advisory on Fake Overseas Job Offers and Cyber Slavery Syndicates
ISSUING AGENCY: Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) & Indian Embassies (e.g., Myanmar, Cambodia, Laos)
DATE OF ISSUE: Current Operating Version

1. OVERVIEW OF THE FRAUD
Unscrupulous agents and illegal placement agencies are trapping Indian job seekers with lucrative offers for data entry jobs, digital marketing roles, or customer support positions in Southeast Asian countries.

2. MODUS OPERANDI
- False Promises: Victims are promised high salaries, free accommodation, and air tickets, often routed through tourist visas instead of valid employment visas.
- Forced Confinement: Upon arrival, passports are confiscated, and victims are forced into cyber slavery syndicates to conduct online financial scams, romance scams, and crypto fraud under severe physical and mental duress.
- Extortion: Traffickers demand heavy ransoms or exit fees from victims' families to secure their release.

3. PREVENTIVE MEASURES & OFFICIAL STANCE
- Always verify the credentials of overseas recruitment agents through the Ministry of External Affairs' e-Migrate portal.
- Avoid accepting job offers that bypass official visa channels or originate from unverified social media solicitations.
- In case of distress abroad, contact the nearest Indian Embassy or Consulate immediately.
