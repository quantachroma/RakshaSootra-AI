DOCUMENT ID: SEBI-I4C-ADV-002
TITLE: Advisory Against Fraudulent Trading Platforms and Stock Market Scams
ISSUING AGENCY: Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) & I4C
DATE OF ISSUE: Current Operating Version

1. OVERVIEW OF THE FRAUD
Fraudsters are luring investors through social media advertisements (WhatsApp, Telegram, Instagram, YouTube) promising guaranteed, abnormally high returns on stock market investments, IPO allotments, and unauthorized Foreign Institutional Trading (FII) accounts.

2. MODUS OPERANDI
- Fake Applications: Victims are tricked into downloading malicious mobile apps or registering on fraudulent web portals that mimic legitimate brokerage platforms (e.g., fake versions of established financial apps).
- Illusion of Profit: The fake dashboards display exorbitant daily gains, encouraging victims to invest increasingly larger sums of money.
- Withdrawal Block: When users attempt to withdraw their funds, they are hit with heavy "taxes," "processing fees," or "commission charges," after which the scammers block communication and vanish.

3. PREVENTIVE MEASURES & OFFICIAL STANCE
- SEBI registered intermediaries never offer guaranteed returns or operate through unauthorized personal bank accounts/payment gateways.
- Verify the registration status of any broker or advisory service on the official SEBI website before investing.
- Report fraudulent investment groups and apps immediately to national cyber authorities.
